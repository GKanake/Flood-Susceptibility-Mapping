USE OF GEOSPATIAL TECHNIQUES TO MAP FLOOD SUSCEPTIBLE AREAS IN TANA DELTA SUB-COUNTY

Project Abstract

This project demonstrates the application of geospatial techniques, that is Remote sensing and Geographical information systems in flood susceptibility mapping in Tana Delta Sub County Area of Study. Tana Delta is chosen as the area of study as the region has experienced annual flood events with flood events reported in Floods reports by Relief web demonstrating that floods are perennial in the region being recorded in 1997/1998 El Nino, May 2003,2006, 2008, March 2010, 2011, 2012 flash floods, 2013 March to May, November 2015 to January 2016, 2018 referred to as Mini El Nino, 2019, May 2020 and May 2021.

The flood events not only cause harm to property and infrastructure but are a threat to human and animal life in the region. Flood conditioning factors of elevation, slope, annual rainfall, river density, distance to rivers, Normalized Difference Vegetation Index, land use, and soil drainage are identified. Corresponding thematic layers of the conditioning factors are produced, reclassified, and evaluated using the Frequency Ratio model in ArcMap for eventual classification according to their contributing effect to flooding in five classes of very low, low, moderate, high, and very high susceptibility.

A final map layout was prepared to show five classes of susceptibility; very low susceptibility (36.16%), low susceptibility (23.27%), moderate susceptibility (21.51%) high susceptibility (9.58%), and very high susceptibility (9.48%). It is however worth noting that most settlements are along River Tana, for which these areas along river tana were most susceptible to flooding adding to the fact that the area receives high conventional rainfall and is also at a very low flat elevation. However, as elevation rises to the west of the sub-county susceptibility level is reduced. Accuracy assessment carried out on the dataset revealed an Overall Accuracy (OA) of 70%.

Objectives

Main Objective

  • To identify and map flood susceptible areas in Tana Delta Sub County.

    • Specific Objectives

  • To produce a flood inventory map of Tana Delta Area.


  • To produce flood susceptibility maps correlating to the conditioning factors.


  • To carry out statistical analysis using frequency ratio model in the production of overall flood susceptibility map.
